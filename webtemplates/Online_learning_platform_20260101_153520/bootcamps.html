Inside the curriculum

Foundation
Weeks 1–4 · Core fundamentals

Get comfortable with programming basics, Git, the command line, and core web concepts. We use interactive exercises, mini‑projects, and frequent code reviews to build confidence.

Hands‑on practice

Daily challenges with instant feedback, guided labs, and pair‑programming ensure you’re applying what you learn rather than passively watching videos.

Projects
Real‑world capstones

Ship 3–4 portfolio‑ready projects that mirror how teams work in production: requirements, design reviews, code reviews, and deployment.

Code quality & teamwork

Learn how to write clean, testable code, collaborate via pull requests, and communicate like an engineer during stand‑ups and retrospectives.

Career prep
Embedded career curriculum

We layer in resume reviews, LinkedIn optimization, networking strategies, and interview drills so you graduate with more than just technical skills.

Mock interviews

Practice live coding, system design, and behavioral interviews with mentors and hiring partners who provide honest, tactical feedback.

Is a bootcamp right for you?

You don’t need a computer science degree. Our beginner‑friendly on‑ramps and interactive lessons are built for teachers, baristas, support reps, and others switching careers.

We’ll help you translate your past experience into strong engineering stories for interviews.

Self‑taught developers

If you’ve been learning from YouTube or tutorials but feel stuck, structured projects, code reviews, and mentor feedback will help you close gaps and build confidence.

You’ll learn industry practices like testing, documentation, and working with teams.

Early‑career professionals

Already working in tech‑adjacent roles like support, QA, or operations? Use a bootcamp to transition into engineering or data roles with a clear plan and portfolio to match.

Career services will guide your internal transfer or external job search strategy.

Bootcamp FAQs

Do I need prior coding experience?

No. Many students start with little or no background. We’ll recommend a beginner on‑ramp if you’re new, or a more advanced cohort if you’ve been learning on your own.

Can I keep my full‑time job while enrolled?

Yes. Our part‑time tracks are designed around working professionals, with evening live sessions and flexible self‑paced work. Expect 10–15 focused hours per week.

What kind of career support do you provide?

You’ll work with a dedicated career coach on resumes, LinkedIn, networking, and interviews. We also connect qualified grads with hiring partners and alumni referrals.

How are mentors selected?

All mentors are senior‑level engineers or data professionals with strong communication skills. We vet for both technical depth and a coaching mindset, not just job titles.

What happens if I fall behind?

Life happens. We’ll work with you on a catch‑up plan, recommend focused study paths, and, in some cases, help you roll into a later cohort if that’s the right move.

How do I know which track to choose?

Start with a short conversation with our team. We’ll map your interests, time constraints, and target roles to the track that gives you the clearest path into tech.
